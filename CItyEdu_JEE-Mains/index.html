The Joint Entrance Examination (JEE) Main consists of two papers: Paper 1 for admission to undergraduate engineering programs and Paper 2 for B.Arch and B.Planning courses. JEE Main 2024 is conducted in two sessions, offering candidates multiple opportunities to improve their scores and reducing the risk of dropping a year due to unforeseen circumstances. Candidates can choose to appear in one or both sessions, with the best scores considered for merit list/ranking preparation.

JEE Main 2024 Eligibility

Candidates who have passed the Class 12 exam in 2022/2023 or who are appearing in 2024 are eligible. There is no age limit for candidates.
Paper Eligibility Criteria
Paper 1 (BE/BTech)
  • Candidates must have studied Physics and Mathematics in Class 12.
  • Additionally, they should have studied any one of Chemistry/Biology/Biotechnology/Technical vocational subjects.
Paper 2A (BArch)
  • Candidates must have studied Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics in Class 12.
Paper 2B (BPlan)
  • Candidates must have studied Mathematics in Class 12.

Note: The 75% criteria has been removed from JEE Exams

JEE Main 2024 Exam Pattern

BTech/BE (Paper 1)

Exam Mode Online (Computer-based)
Number of Sections and Subjects 3 (Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics)
Duration of Exam 3 hours (180 minutes), 4 hours for PwD candidates
Section A (MCQ)
  • Mathematics: 20 questions
  • Physics: 20 questions
  • Chemistry: 20 questions
Section B (Numerical Value)
  • Mathematics: 10 questions
  • Physics: 10 questions
  • Chemistry: 10 questions
Candidates have to attempt any five questions out of 10 in Section B.
Total Number of Questions 90
Total Marks 300 marks

BArch and BPlan (Paper 2)

Exam Mode Online (Mathematics and Aptitude) and offline (Drawing test) for BArch; Online for BPlanning
Number of Sections and Subjects 3 (Drawing, Mathematics, and Aptitude) for BArch; 3 (Mathematics, Aptitude, and Planning-Based Questions) for BPlanning
Duration of Exam 3 hours (180 minutes) for both BArch and BPlanning
Total Number of Questions
  • BArch: 82
  • BPlanning: 105
Total Marks 400 marks for both BArch and BPlanning

Key Features & Benefits of JEE Main

  • It provides access to top engineering institutions, including NITs, IIITs, and other centrally funded technical institutions across India.
  • After the exam, a centralized counseling process allows students to select their preferred colleges and courses based on their scores.
  • JEE Main offers a more economical option for students, reducing the financial burden associated with multiple applications.
  • This exam eliminates the need for separate entrance exams for different colleges, simplifying the admission procedure and saving students valuable time and effort.
  • JEE Main scores can also be utilized for lateral entry into some engineering colleges in other states.
