About MBBS

The first choice of any aspiring medical candidate ambitious to become a doctor is getting an MBBS degree. Bachelor of Medicine and Bachelor of Surgery or MBBS is a 5.5-year undergraduate medical course offered to students having a science background with Biology as a compulsory subject in 10+2 education. There are 600+ medical colleges in India with 90000+ MBBS seats out of which there are 300+ government medical colleges with nearly 45000+ MBBS seats recognized by the National Medical Commission of India. Admissions to MBBS in India are based on clearing the NEET-UG with a qualifying cut-off score as accepted by the colleges. Some of the top states with the highest number of medical seats are Maharashtra, Karnataka, West Bengal, Tamil Nadu, Telangana, Rajasthan, and Gujrat.

mbbs Eligibility Criteria

  • The candidate must have completed 12th/2nd PUC with Physics,Chemistry & Biology as mandatory subjects .
  • The candidates should Mandatorily Qualified in NEET UG Entrance Exam.
  • The candidate must have completed 18years by the time of admission

MBBS ADMISSION PROCESS

  • Based on NEET scores, counseling sessions are conducted by state authorities or the Medical Counseling Committee (MCC) at the national level.
  • During counseling, candidates can choose and prioritize their preferred medical colleges and courses based on their NEET ranks
  • After counseling, candidates are allotted seats in medical colleges according to their NEET rank, choices filled during counseling, and availability of seats.
  • Once a seat is allotted, candidates are required to verify their documents and submit the necessary certificates to confirm their admission.
  • After document verification, candidates need to pay the admission fees to secure their seats.
  • The final admission process may include additional procedures set by the respective medical colleges.

    • * The Above process is subjected to change according to any new government rules and policy*
