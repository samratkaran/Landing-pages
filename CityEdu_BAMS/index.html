BAMS Overview

Bachelor of Ayurvedic Medicine and Surgery (BAMS) is an undergraduate degree program in India, focusing on Ayurveda, a traditional system of medicine. BAMS integrates Ayurvedic principles with modern medical science, equipping students to become Ayurvedic doctors and practitioners. The course duration is typically five and a half years. To enroll in BAMS, candidates must have completed Class 12 in the Science stream with Physics, Chemistry, and Biology (PCB) subjects. Additionally, clearing the National Eligibility cum Entrance Test (NEET-UG) is mandatory for admission to BAMS courses.


BAMS graduates have diverse career opportunities, ranging from practicing as Ayurvedic doctors to working in research, education, and consultancy roles. The course equips students with knowledge of both ancient healing techniques and contemporary medical practices, making them versatile healthcare professionals. The average salary range for BAMS graduates reflects the demand for their expertise, offering lucrative prospects for career growth and financial stability.

BAMS Eligibility Criteria

  • To be eligible for BAMS courses, candidates must have completed Class 12 in the Science stream with Physics, Chemistry, and Biology (PCB) subjects.
  • The age of the candidate should be 18 at the time of admission.
  • Candidates must pass the National Eligibility cum Entrance Test (NEET) for admission.
  • BAMS admissions are based on NEET scores through centralised counselling.

BAMS Admission Process

The admission process for BAMS (Bachelor of Ayurvedic Medicine and Surgery) typically involves several steps:
Stage Description
Meeting Eligibility Criteria Candidates must complete Class 12 education with Science stream (Physics, Chemistry, Biology) and achieve a minimum percentage (usually 50% to 60%).
Appearing for Entrance Exams Candidates must appear for NEET entrance exams accepted for BAMS admission.
Counseling Sessions Counseling sessions are conducted by state authorities or centralized counseling bodies like the Ayush Admissions Central Counseling Committee (AACCC) based on NEET scores. During counseling, candidates prioritize their preferred colleges and courses based on NEET ranks.
Seat Allotment Seats are allotted based on NEET rank, preferences filled during counseling, and seat availability in chosen colleges.
Document Verification Candidates undergo document verification at designated centers, submitting original documents and certificates for verification.
Payment of Admission Fees Once documents are verified, candidates pay prescribed admission fees to confirm their seats in allotted colleges.
Final Admission Upon successful completion of document verification and fee payment, candidates secure admission to the BAMS program in chosen colleges.
