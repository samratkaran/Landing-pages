What is a Bachelor of Dental Surgery

Bachelor of Dental Surgery or BDS is a 5 years undergraduate course for aspirants of dental education. The 5 years of the course are divided into 4 years of academic learning and a 1 year compulsory rotational/non-rotational internship to earn the degree. The graduates are titled Doctors and are an on-demand career option for NEET aspirants.

The four-year classroom (Core, elective subjects, and Practicals) program covers a vast syllabus of General and Dental Pharmacology, Pathology, Dental Therapeutics, Oral Microbiology and Pathology, and Preclinical Dentistry.

Eligibility for Studying BDS in India

To be eligible to study BDS in India, the candidate must fulfill the following basic criteria:

  • The candidate must be an Indian Nationals/Overseas Citizen of India (OCI)/Non-Resident Indian (NRI)/Person of Indian Origin (PIO)
  • The candidate must have passed 10+2 or equivalent examination (must have studied for 12 years) from a recognized board or university with a minimum aggregate of 50% for Unreserved candidates/NRI/OCI/foreign candidates, 45% for SC/ST/OBC.
  • The candidate must be of 17 years of age as of 31st December of the year of admission

FUTURE SCOPE OF DENTAL EDUCATION

Dental Health has prevailed as a major healthcare issue over decades and people being more health cautious in the recent era are more interested to take care of their oral health. In India, the ratio of the total dentists to the total population of India calculates to 1 dentist for every 5000 people which marks the huge lack of dentists and oral health professionals in the country. The future scope alongside the rapid adaptation of technology offers an extensive career option for BDS graduates.

What after BDS?

Job/Employment

A fresh BDS graduate has multiple employment areas like Dental Clinics, Private or Government Hospitals, NGOs, and more. The average salary for fresh BDS graduates in India is somewhere from Rs.4 lakhs to Rs. 5 lakhs which keeps on increasing with experience. The government sector offers multiple clinical and non-clinical roles for BDS graduates after clearing govt. entrance exams like UPSC, SPSC, or SSC.

Self-Practitioner or Private Clinic

A BDS graduate after getting registered with the Dental Council of India can start with their own private clinic as a dental doctor starting with basic oral healthcare services like scaling, extraction, tooth restorations, partial dental removals, etc.

Higher Education

BDS graduates can also choose to study further instead of starting with a job career right after graduating. India offers a high-end education for Masters and Doctoral courses after BDS with some of the most in-demand courses like Master in Dental Surgery, Master in Public Health, and even go for Ph.D. Being a research fellow can offer the most highest-paid job in the oral health industry.

Lecturers

Another prominent career option after BDS is to go complete an MDS degree and become a lecturer. Most of the premium dental institutes and hospitals offer a great career as a lecturer or professional in the dental department.

ADMISSION & SELECTION PROCESS

The admissions to all 300+ dental colleges in India are based on the state or central-based NEET counseling process. NEET UG or National Eligibility cum Entrance Test is a common dental entrance exam in India. The state or central-based BDS Counselling 2023 is based on Round 1 Counselling, Round 2 Counselling, followed by the Mop-up round and Stray Vacancy Round.
